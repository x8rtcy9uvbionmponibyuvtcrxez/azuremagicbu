The loophole:

There’s a big misconception that these inboxes are special in one way or another.

Words like “enterprise” or “azure” get thrown around.

But they’re simply just Outlook shared mailboxes.

Shared mailboxes are essentially standard outlook mailboxes, just with limited functionality. Meaning they’re only purpose is to send and receive emails.

They’re primary use case is for collaborative accounts like info@ or support@ inboxes, but the key loophole is you can actually assign passwords to these mailboxes which allows you to connect them to your sender to send and receive emails.

And most importantly, they DON'T need a paid license/subscription to send and receive emails.

So you only need to pay $4.50/m for 1 Exchange Online license -> assign it to 1 regular user mailbox and use the other 99 inboxes for “free”.

But the tricky part, is being able to setup working infrastructure without running into major issues or getting flagged with Microsoft.

The system is essentially a puzzle, where every piece is crucial and without one - the whole system breaks.

Note on “Azure inboxes”:

These are NOT ‘Azure Inboxes’. There’s technically no such thing as Azure Inboxes.

Azure is simply a hosting provider owned by Microsoft.

What you would consider azure inboxes are rare, and are actually just private SMTP servers hosted on azure with a custom email server hosted on it.

That’s an entirely different system to this one.

Deliverability

Deliverability in terms of infrastructure (not including email copy or other little factors) comes down to 2 things:

  1. Domain health/reputation
  2. Server health/reputation

Health or reputation essentially means - how do other major ESPs such as Google or Microsoft view my domain or server? Do they recognise it as spam or safe?

Good health = Google, Microsoft etc. don’t view your domain or server as spam.

And vice versa

So when it comes to these inboxes (and Google) your only consideration is domain health.

Because Google and Microsoft have such a major infrastructure that’s used by majority of the population, that their servers are essentially “bulletproof” and cannot have a bad reputation.

That’s what gives Google and Microsoft inboxes a massive leg up over private SMTP inboxes.

Therefore if deliverability is poor, and all other factors are considered - it’s likely your domain health.

Costs

~$4.50/m per license = 1 domain & tenant x 99 inboxes

Limits & Considerations

As you already know, Microsoft does not like people misusing their platform. Which is why you need to take measures to ensure you don’t get flagged.

There are limits.

  1. Tenant sending threshold:
  1. Every new tenant has a “hidden sending limit” which is around 2,000 emails/day (across ALL inboxes). This includes cold emails, warmup emails and warmup replies.
  2. This is the reasoning behind the warmup and sending settings we recommend.
  1. Never have inboxes warming up on more than 1 platform at a time.
  1. For example if you had inboxes connected to instantly and emailbison, with warmup enabled in both that would be a big NO NO.
  1. New tenants have a 3 day “warmup period”.
  1. Another hidden limit from Microsoft. Within three days of setting up a tenant, if you attempt to connect inboxes to a sender -> and send an email, you’ll likely hit an IP error.
  2. You simply just need to wait out the 3 days and it will resolve itself.

Risk Mitigation (IMPORTANT)

  1. Random licenses:

For every 10 - 20 tenants you, set up 1 - 2 with random licenses other than just Exchange Online Plan 1.

These can be any licenses, but the point is to show you’re not just solely subscribing to the cheapest product that allows you to send emails - this screams spam.

  1. Spread across tenant providers:

All of your tenants should be spread across at least 2 tenant providers. For example if you need 100 tenants - setup 50 on Intermedia and 50 on Sherweb.

Don’t just stick to Intermedia and Sherweb however, there are plenty more CSPs.

One-time setup

The following steps take you through setting up the tools and systems necessary to build your infrastructure. They only require a one-time setup and will just need to be maintained with any future updates or if something breaks.

Tenant provider:

A tenant provider (or CSP) is a third party platform that allows you to purchase and set up a high volume of tenants without dealing with major billing headaches that come with buying licenses directly from Microsoft.

Why you need it:

You’ll likely be setting up a high number of tenants, so going through Microsoft's direct signup can be cumbersome and have greater risk of your tenant/s getting banned.

Unfortunately, getting setup with these CSPs is not as easy as signing up and entering your credit card.

These providers allow you to sign up without going through Microsoft’s painful reseller sign up process:

  • Sherweb
  • Select white-label option during registration
  • Will likely require govt ID
  • Intermedia
  • We can provide a referral signup link

There is also Pax8, however they require you to get verified with Microsoft first.

It’s important to understand the risks that come with using these 3rd parties as they can ban your entire partner account (and the tenants with it) at any point.

Although if you’re setting up a small volume of tenants (100 - 200) then you’re in a safe range.

Best practice would be to use multiple LLCs, phone numbers, credit cards and aliases across platforms to manage risk.

Airtable Setup:

Airtable will serve as the central hub for this entire operation. It’s where you’ll store and track all domains, tenants and credentials.

Why you need it:

When managing dozens of domains and thousands of inboxes, you need a centralized system to track credentials, domain status, and configuration details.

Without Airtable, you would need to manually record this information in messy spreadsheets and manually update scripts with credentials every time you want to create inboxes.

Airtable's API integration allows scripts to automatically retrieve the correct credentials for each domain, update status fields when tasks complete, and store important information like Tenant IDs and Cloudflare Zone IDs for future reference.

Cloudflare Setup:

Cloudflare will be the platform where you host all of your domains, to allow you to easily manage all of the DNS records and redirects automatically.

What it does:

Cloudflare hosts all the DNS records for your domains, including MX records for email delivery, SPF/DKIM/DMARC records for email authentication, and A records for website forwarding.

The automation scripts communicate directly with Cloudflare's API to automatically configure all these DNS settings when you add a new domain.

Why you need it:

Manually configuring DNS records for every domain would take hours. Cloudflare's API allows the scripts to automatically create all necessary DNS records in seconds. Additionally, Cloudflare provides website forwarding so your domains can redirect to any URL you choose.

  1. Sign up at: https://dash.cloudflare.com/sign-up
  2. Skip the onboarding and go straight to: https://dash.cloudflare.com/profile/api-tokens
  3. Click View for Global API Key, verify your email then copy the key and store it for later.

RDP Setup:

RDPs (Remote Desktop Protocol) are virtual Windows computers hosted in data centers that you can connect to remotely from your own device. Think of it as renting a computer in the cloud that runs 24/7.

What it does:

An RDP provides a clean, dedicated Windows environment where you can run all the automation scripts without affecting your personal computer. It acts as the "command center" where all domain and inbox creation happens.

Why you need it:

Microsoft monitors login patterns and device fingerprints. If you create dozens of Microsoft 365 tenants from your personal computer or home network, Microsoft will flag your activity as suspicious and potentially ban your accounts.

RDPs provide a consistent, US-based IP address and clean device

fingerprint that keeps Microsoft from detecting automation patterns. Additionally, RDPs allow the scripts to run continuously without keeping your personal computer on.

RDPs can be rented on a subscription from a bunch of hosting providers, but we recommend Contabo as they give you the best bang for your buck.

RDP Setup (Powershell):

Powershell is the main application that makes this system possible. It manages the entire domain and inbox setup process.

What it does:

PowerShell runs all the automation scripts that create Microsoft 365 tenants, add domains, create users, set up mailboxes, configure passwords, and manage permissions.

It connects directly to Microsoft's cloud services through special modules (ExchangeOnlineManagement and Microsoft.Graph) that give it the ability to perform administrative tasks programmatically.

Why you need it:

Creating 99 inboxes manually through Microsoft's web interface would take 6-8 hours per domain and be extremely error-prone. PowerShell scripts automate this entire process down to 30 minutes per domain with zero manual clicking.

How it works:

We provide you with pre-written PowerShell scripts (files ending in .ps1) that contain sequences of commands. When you run a script, PowerShell executes each command in order: connecting to Microsoft 365, authenticating with your Azure App credentials, creating mailboxes, setting passwords, updating DNS records through Python helper scripts, and tracking progress in Airtable.

The scripts handle all the complex API calls, error handling, and retry logic automatically. You simply run the script, provide the domain name when prompted, and PowerShell does all the work.

Azure app setup:

The Azure App (also called App Registration or Service Principal) is the authentication bridge that allows the PowerShell scripts to automatically connect with your Microsoft 365 tenants without manual login.

What it does:

  • Acts as a "robot user" that can manage domains, users, and mailboxes across all your Microsoft 365 tenants
  • Provides the scripts with permission to perform actions like adding domains, creating users, updating passwords, and managing mailboxes
  • Uses API credentials (Client ID and Client Secret) instead of requiring you to manually log in each time

Why you need it:

Without the Azure App, you would have to manually log into each tenant every single time you want to create mailboxes or manage domains. The Azure App enables complete automation by giving the scripts the necessary permissions to work on your behalf.

How it works:

  1. You create one Azure App in your main Microsoft account
  2. The scripts use the App's credentials (Client ID: 35bda794-fd06-4e53-b935-26170f9eb12e and Client Secret) to authenticate
  3. This App is granted specific permissions to manage Microsoft 365 resources via Microsoft Graph API
  4. The same App credentials work across all your tenants, making the process scalable

Tenant Setup

The entire tenant setup process can be broken down into 3 stages:

  1. Gathering tenant info (creating business names, addresses etc.)

  1. Creating tenants (creating tenants through your CSP)

  1. Prepping tenants (preparing the tenants for use - MUST BE DONE WITHIN 24 HOURS OF TENANT CREATION)

Stage 1: Gathering tenant info:

Creating tenants requires semi-legitimate business information. Microsoft requires the following:

  • Business name
  • REAL and verifiable address
  • Phone number
  • Contact person name
  • Domain

Business name and person name generation:

For business names and person names we created a claude project to generate names for us with the following project instructions:

You have 2 jobs:

1. Generating batches of completely unique business names, that sound like real companies BUT are not real companies.

They should sound like real legacy industries, like manufacturing, finance, medicine, logistics, insurance etc.

Avoid using made up words to sound tech fancy

There should be no duplicates to those business names provided in the files or in lists provided.

2. Generating unique person names, first and last name across all cultures.

There should be no duplicates to those names provided in the files or in lists provided.

Ensure there are absolutely no '-' characters in the names or any special characters of any kind

Then I just open a chat in the project with the following prompts:

Business names:

please generate 50 more business names. ensuring there are no duplicates with those in the files and the previous 20 generated

dont just generate names with 3 words. some should have 2, or 3 or even 4

Person names:

generate 50 unique person names in CSV format ensuring there are no duplicates with those in the names list in files and the 20 previously generated

And absolutely no '-' characters in the names or any special characters of any kind

Domain:

Microsoft requires a default ‘domain’ during tenant creation. This is NOT the custom sending domain you’ll use in your outreach.

It’s just a default domain that comes before ‘.onmicrosft.com’ (default microsoft domain) like ‘emberlandingstudio.onmicrosoft.com’

It can be absolutely anything, but it MUST be unique as duplicates will fail during the creation process.

We like to take the contact name and use that.

Finding real addresses:

For addresses, it’s crucial you use real addresses as Microsoft verifies each one during the tenant creation process. You can download databases of real addresses in the US, just ask ChatGPT or any LLM to point you in the right direction.

Combining tenant information:

Combine all of the information, business names, person names and addresses into a spreadsheet so you can keep track of all of them and wont have duplicates.

Mailbox Setup

The entire mailbox setup process can be broken down into 3 stages:

  1. Setting up the domains (adding to cloudflare, automated DNS setup etc.)
  2. Adding the domains to the tenant
  3. Creating and configuring the inboxes

Each stage must be repeated every time you want to spin up new mailboxes.

Stage 1: Setting up the domains

By this point you should have already imported your tenants into airtable along with their assigned domains, forwarding URLs and inbox names.

Important considerations:

Each domain must be unique for each tenant. The forwarding URL must include ‘https://’ in order for it work properly, like - https://mailr.io

  1. Open command prompt inside the RDP, paste this command and hit enter:

cd "C:\Users\Administrator\Desktop\Powershell-Scripts”

  1. Paste this command and hit enter (you can also run this script on your own desktop as we’re only working with cloudflare and airtable):

        python addzone_airtable.py

This script will search airtable for all domains that have empty Nameservers -> add them to Cloudflare -> update the nameservers in airtable -> and configure most of the DNS records.



You’ll need to run this script anytime you add new domains to airtable.

Ultimately, you can customise these automations in any way you want to make it even more streamlined.

For example, our user dashboard would automatically add each domain that users order to cloudflare and return the nameservers.

  1. Update the nameservers in your registrar, then the domains should become active in Cloudflare within 5 - 15 minutes.

        

Just open Cloudflare and you can search for your domain/s to check if they’re active.

  1. Once the domains are active, and there were no issues with configuration you can proceed to Stage 2 - adding the domain to the tenant.

Stage 2: Adding the domain to the tenant

At the moment, you’ll have to go through these steps for each domain you want to setup.

This stage involves adding the domain to the assigned tenant.

It’s almost entirely automated, and will also create the licensed user.

  1. Enter the tenant name into the interface and click run

You’ll also see in https://admin.cloud.microsoft/?#/users there’s now 1 active user under the domain, with the Exchange Online license. It will have whatever name under Alias in airtable.

This concludes Stage 2, and you can immediately move onto Stage 3 if there are no issues.
